Badshahi Masjid

From the grandeur of Badshahi Mosque to the heights of Minar-e-Pakistan, the famous places of Lahore define its historical skyline and architectural soul.

Badshahi Mosque is one of the largest mosques in the world. It was built in 1673 by Mughal Emperor Aurangzeb Alamgir. The mosque is located near the historic Lahore Fort. It is made of red sandstone with white marble decorations. The mosque has three large domes and four tall minarets. Its courtyard can accommodate more than 100,000 worshippers. Badshahi Mosque is an important religious and cultural symbol of Pakistan. It attracts thousands of tourists and worshippers every day.

Wagah Border

Wagah Border is the famous border crossing between Pakistan and India near Lahore. It is located about 30 kilometers from the center of Lahore. Wagah Border is known for its daily flag-lowering ceremony. The ceremony is performed by soldiers of both countries with great energy and discipline. Thousands of visitors come every day to watch this patriotic event. The event shows national pride, unity, and respect for the country. Wagah Border has become a major tourist attraction in Lahore. It represents the strong spirit and patriotism of the people of Pakistan.

Shahi Qila

Experience the grandeur of Mughal history at Shahi Qila (Lahore Fort), where magnificent palaces, royal gates, and centuries-old architecture reflect the power and elegance of the Mughal Empire

Shalimar Gardens is a historic Mughal garden located in Lahore, Pakistan. It was built in 1641 by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan. The garden was designed to show the beauty and power of Mughal architecture. It consists of three terraces, each at a different level. The gardens include fountains, pools, marble pavilions, and lush greenery. Water flows through an advanced hydraulic system created during the Mughal era. Shalimar Gardens is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is one of the most visited historical and tourist places in Lahore.

Minar-e-Pakistan

From the grandeur of Badshahi Mosque to the heights of Minar-e-Pakistan, the famous places of Lahore define its historical skyline and architectural soul.

Minar-e-Pakistan is a national monument located in Greater Iqbal Park, Lahore. It was built to remember the Lahore Resolution of 1940. The resolution led to the creation of Pakistan. Construction started in 1960 and was completed in 1968. The minar is about 70 meters high. Its design combines Islamic, Mughal, and modern architecture. Minar-e-Pakistan represents freedom, unity, and independence. It is one of the most popular tourist attractions in Lahore
